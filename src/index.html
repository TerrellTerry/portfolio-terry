Did We Brick It?

E-Commerce web application
The Did We Brick It? E-Commerce Web Application provides the core features present in many common e-commerce applications. General functionality includes features such as creating and logging into an account, viewing products and a profile, as well as adding items to a cart and checking out.

This project was created at my time in Revature as a larger scale website with a team of 14 people. This was a project created as part of my training. I was responsible for creating portions of the profile page and the ability to create new products. As a team, we also created a presentation and presented it to multiple people within Revature. Daycare Report Card System

Full Stack Microservices Application
The Daycare Report Card System is an application for recording the behavior of students at a local daycare center. Teachers can make grades for students throughout the day noting if they have been behaving well or not. Guardians can login to view their children's grades. Grades and students can be deleted by teachers.

This was another project created as part of my training at Revature. This project was made with a team of 4 people where we all assisted each other creating this website. I was responsible for creating the services that communicated with our backend and creating the "get all students" functionality.

The theme was Team Titans and so we tried to fit that style.

Town Review Board

Full Stack Web Application
The Town Review board is an application for a small local town. The application allows constituents to submit issues that they have noticed in the town. The review board members can look over these issues. Board members can then create meetings which can be viewed by constituents and display information on what needs to be discussed.

This is another application created as part of my training. I created the website myself, utilizing the skills and abilities taught during training. This used technology that was not previously known to me before and was my first attempt at a full stack application.

The theme for this website was Hawkins from Stranger Things and so I tried to fit that style.

Final Paradise

A top-down roguelike based on the game Binding of Isaac created by a team of 4 people for my capstone project at Full Sail University. Within the game, the player traverses 6 levels, all with a boss room, shop room and treasure room. There are locked rooms within each area as well. This game does not have procedural generation, a feature that proved to be too complex at the time.

This was a very difficult project to complete, as we started with 5 team members and by the end of it, we had 4 members left. This project taught me a lot about who I was as a game developer and what good team work really looks like. I spent countless nights up until 3-4 in the morning, trying to make sure that we had a presentable project from the week before. One other team member was helping me in these nights, and we started working on games together after university.

Trailer: https://youtu.be/K_hCgpDzbFY

Game Engine: Unity

SCRAPS Level Design

A game where I worked on a level as part of a team from Full Sail University. The level created was to obtain a golden honeybun and give it to the bastions (the quest givers). Within my level, I utilized the introduce, practice, master design patterns for introducing new mechanics to the player. The player goes through the level by destroying breakable walls and using a pressure plate mechanic.

The level was inspired by my workplace, as I worked in a honeybun factory and I wanted to create an environment that felt similar to my workplace.

My own critique would be that I could have created the levels with more thought-out detail and mechanics and tried to do something more with them. At the time, I was going through a rough time and tried to do the best with what I was given. That is not to dismiss the faults within the level design, but I believe that the levels that I will be designing in my newest game will surpass them completely.

Video of the Level: https://youtu.be/E6d12wV0wf8?t=851 (Time stamp: 14:10).
Game Engine: Unity

Project Lazy God

A top-down tower defense game where you play as the enemies that are preventing the heros from getting into your dungeon and stealing your resources, similar to the Dungeons series. From within the game, you are able to change and augment your boss, change and shape the terrain and place down towers on a grid. When the level starts, the enemies will start to navigate towards the crystal that the player needs to protect. The player's goal is to fight off the waves of enemies and build up their base.

Within Sneezing Panda Studios, we had over 10 members who were working on the game. We tried to take a hobbyist approach to it, working on things when we had time, but we quickly realized that wasn't really working. So eventually we had to disband the project.
